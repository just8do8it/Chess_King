Solving the 3x3x3 Rubik's cube

Solving the Rubik's cube seems really complicated at first,
but once you start getting into it you realise it is pretty
easy, you just have to put some time and work.
There are a lot of ways to solve the 3x3x3 cube (the standart cube),
but for starters their is a easy begginer's method,
which involves only a few algorithms and you can learn
it in days. Here is a great video describing it:

The most important thing when solving the cube is how much
time does it take you to do it. Speedcubing (also known as speedsolving) is the
hobby involving solving a variety of twisty puzzles, the most famous being the 3x3
puzzle or Rubik's Cube, as quickly as possible. The need of solving the cube fast
has made it's way out in the form of faster cubes, and also faster methods for
solving them. The most popular method for speedcubing is called CFOP, standing for
Cross, F2L(first two layers), OLL(orienting last layer) and PLL(permuting last layer).
This method is complicated, because it takes a lot of practise to learn all the
necessary algorithms, and there are 30+ of them. Along the way you learn shortcuts
here and there and you might even invent an algorithm yourself. Because this method is
hard to learn, it can't really be explained in one video, so click the link below to see
a playlist of tutorials for CFOP.
